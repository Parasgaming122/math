Time Management

Paper 1 Overview

Duration: 1 hour 45 minutes (105 minutes)  |  Total marks: 75  |  Questions: ~10-12

Golden rule: Spend roughly 1.4 minutes per mark. A 6-mark question should take ~8 minutes.

Command Word Definitions

Show that...
The answer is GIVEN. You must show every step of working to arrive at it. If you skip steps, you lose marks even if the answer is correct.
Hence...
You MUST use the result from the previous part. Using a different method earns 0 marks.
Hence or otherwise...
You CAN use the previous result (recommended!) or use a different valid method.
State...
Give the answer without working. Usually 1 mark. Just write the value/result directly.
Determine...
Find the answer showing all necessary working. Similar to "Find" but may require justification.
Find...
Calculate the answer showing working. Most common instruction in P1.
Sketch...
Draw an approximate graph showing key features (intercepts, asymptotes, shape). Not to scale, but must be clear.
Exact value...
Leave answer in surd/fraction/$\pi$ form. Do NOT use decimals. E.g., $\frac{3\sqrt{2}}{4}$ not $1.06$.

Mark Scheme Conventions

Types of Marks

MarkNameWhat it meansCan you get it alone?
M Method Awarded for a correct method, even if the answer is wrong Yes — show the method
A Accuracy Awarded for a correct answer, depends on getting M first No — needs M mark first
B Independent Awarded independently of other marks (e.g., stating a value) Yes — standalone mark
Key insight: Even if your final answer is wrong, you can still get M marks for correct method. ALWAYS show your working — never just write the answer!

What Loses Marks

  • No working shown — even for "easy" steps, show them
  • Premature rounding — keep full precision until the final answer
  • Missing units — if the question has units (cm, cm²), include them
  • Not answering what's asked — read the question again before writing your answer
  • Crossing out correct work — if you're unsure, don't cross it out

Common Mistakes by Topic

Quadratics

1
Completing the square: Forgetting to multiply the constant back by $a$ after factoring it out.
2
Inequalities: Dividing by a negative without flipping the inequality sign.
3
Discriminant: Using $b^2 + 4ac$ instead of $b^2 - 4ac$.

Functions

1
Inverse: Forgetting to state the domain of $f^{-1}$ (it's the range of $f$).
2
Composite: Computing $fg(x)$ as $f(x) \cdot g(x)$ instead of $f(g(x))$.
3
Transformations: Getting the direction of horizontal shifts wrong ($f(x+a)$ shifts LEFT, not right).

Trigonometry

1
Missing solutions: Only finding one solution when CAST gives two (or more) in range.
2
Calculator mode: Calculator in degrees when question uses radians (or vice versa).
3
R-formula: Writing $y = 2\sin(3x-90°)$ with shift $90°$ instead of correctly factoring to $2\sin(3(x-30°))$ → shift is $30°$.

Differentiation & Integration

1
Chain rule: Forgetting to multiply by the derivative of the inner function. $\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(3x)] = 3\cos(3x)$, NOT $\cos(3x)$.
2
Integration constant: Forgetting $+C$ in indefinite integrals.
3
Area below axis: Not taking the absolute value when the curve is below the $x$-axis.

How to Write Solutions

  1. One step per line — don't cram multiple steps onto one line
  2. Use "=" signs properly — each line should follow logically from the previous
  3. State formulas before substituting — write $S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n-1)d]$ then substitute
  4. Label your answers clearly — underline or box your final answer
  5. For "show that" questions — work from what you're given toward what you need to show. Never write the result first.
  6. Draw diagrams — for coordinate geometry, circular measure, and trig questions, a sketch helps
  7. Give exact values when asked — leave as fractions, surds, or in terms of $\pi$
  8. Use correct notation — $\frac{dy}{dx}$ not $dy/dx$, proper integral signs, etc.

What's Given vs. What to Memorise

Given in the Formula Sheet (MF19)

  • Binomial expansion formula
  • AP: $u_n$ and $S_n$ formulas
  • GP: $u_n$, $S_n$, and $S_\infty$ formulas
  • Arc length: $s = r\theta$
  • Sector area: $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$
  • Standard derivatives table
  • Standard integrals table

Must Memorise

  • Completing the square method
  • Quadratic formula and discriminant
  • Coordinate geometry formulas (gradient, distance, midpoint)
  • Circle equation and converting forms
  • Trig identities ($\sin^2+\cos^2=1$, $\tan=\sin/\cos$)
  • CAST diagram
  • Segment area = Sector − Triangle
  • Chain rule, product rule, quotient rule
  • Volume of revolution formulas
  • Trapezium rule

Exam Day Checklist

What to Bring

  • Scientific calculator (check batteries!)
  • Spare calculator if possible
  • Black pen (required), pencil for graphs
  • Ruler, eraser, pencil sharpener
  • Watch (phones not allowed)
  • Water bottle
  • Your candidate number and centre number

Calculator Setup

  • Make sure it's in DEG mode (default for most P1 questions)
  • Know how to switch between DEG and RAD quickly
  • Practice using the $x^y$, $\sqrt{}$, and fraction buttons
  • Know where the $\pi$ button is
  • Clear memory before the exam
